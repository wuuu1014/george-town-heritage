Route A: Traditional Craftsmanship Corridor

Route Overview: This module explores the geographic distribution and cultural significance of endangered traditional craftsmanship in George Town. By examining the living heritage sites, this linear spatial corridor visualizes how multi-ethnic artisan workshops coexist within the historic urban landscape and highlights the pressing challenges of cultural transmission in a changing modern society.

💡 Tip: Click any heritage card below to dynamically focus the spatial map directly on the artisan site.

1. Penang Heritage Joss Stick Maker
李明泉手工制香 Active / Declining

Est. Year: c. 1948 | Category: Traditional Craft / Joss Stick Making

Founded by the late Lee Beng Chuan around 1948, this workshop continues George Town’s handmade joss stick tradition near the Goddess of Mercy Temple. After Lee passed away in 2020, his son Lee Chin Poh inherited the craft and continues the business.

2. Hong Kong Shoe Store
香港鞋店 Active / Declining

Est. Year: c. 1958 | Category: Traditional Craft / Handmade Shoemaking

Established by Wong Sung Choy around 1958, Hong Kong Shoe Store is known for handmade and custom-made shoes, including Baba Nyonya beaded shoes, dance shoes, prosthetic shoes and other special designs. It represents George Town’s small-scale urban craftsmanship.

3. Ironsmith Sculpture & Memory Site
打铁匠雕塑 / 打铁街记忆点 Memory Site

Est. Year: Unknown | Category: Traditional Trade Memory / Ironworks

This stop uses the Ironsmith steel-rod sculpture as a memory point for George Town’s former ironworks trade. The sculpture marks Toh Aka Lane, once locally remembered as an “ironworks street” associated with ironsmiths and working-class urban craft.

4. Kedai Songkok OSM Mohd Shariff
OSM Mohd Shariff 宋谷帽制作铺 Active

Est. Year: 1936 | Category: Traditional Craft / Songkok Making

Operating inside the historic enclave, this shop highlights the Muslim community's traditional hat-making craft (Songkok). Serving generations of locals, it represents multi-ethnic heritage coexistence along the historic streets.

Route B: Cultural & Religious Diversity

Route Overview: This module maps the spatial concentration of diverse architectural landmarks representing harmonious coexistence of multicultural communities in Penang. It highlights how religious sites form historical nodes of cultural identity, illustrating multi-ethnic adaptation within the regional geography.

💡 Tip: Click any heritage card below to dynamically focus the spatial map directly on the landmark.

1. Goddess of Mercy Temple
广东观音亭 / 观音亭 Active Religious Site

Est. Year: 1800 | Category: Religious Heritage / Chinese Temple

The Goddess of Mercy Temple, also known as Kuan Yin Teng, is one of the oldest Chinese temples in Penang. Located on Jalan Masjid Kapitan Keling, it represents Chinese religious practice and remains an active worship space in George Town’s multicultural heritage core.

2. Kapitan Keling Mosque
吉宁甲必丹清真寺 Active Religious Site

Est. Year: 1801 | Category: Religious Heritage / South Indian Muslim Mosque

Kapitan Keling Mosque was built by South Indian Muslim traders in the early nineteenth century. Located near other major religious landmarks, it reflects the Tamil Muslim community’s role in George Town’s trading history and the city’s long tradition of religious coexistence.

3. Sri Mahamariamman Temple
马哈马里安曼印度庙 Active Religious Site

Est. Year: 1833 | Category: Religious Heritage / Hindu Temple

Sri Mahamariamman Temple is the oldest Hindu temple in Penang. Located in Little India, it is closely connected with the South Indian Tamil community and is known for its colourful Dravidian-style entrance tower and continuing role in Hindu worship and festivals.

4. St. George’s Church
圣乔治教堂 Active Religious Site

Est. Year: 1818 / 1819 | Category: Religious Heritage / Anglican Church

St. George’s Church is a nineteenth-century Anglican church in George Town and is widely recognised as one of the oldest Anglican churches in Southeast Asia. Its Georgian-style architecture and colonial history add a Christian and British colonial narrative to the town.
