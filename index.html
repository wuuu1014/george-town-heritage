🇲🇾 Mapping Living Heritage in George Town
🗺️ Interactive Heritage Map 📖 Cultural Narrative 📊 Open Dataset

Interactive Heritage Map

1. Research Purpose & Questions

George Town is well known for its rich cultural heritage and multicultural history. This project explores a selection of heritage landmarks across George Town and other parts of Penang to better understand how different communities, historical events, and cultural traditions have shaped the island over time.

The project focuses on two core research questions. The first question looks at where these heritage sites are located and whether there are any visible spatial patterns. The second question explores the stories and meanings associated with these places based on information collected from official heritage sources and community narratives.

2. Methodology & Process

To answer these questions, our group collected information on a range of heritage sites, including religious buildings, clan houses, colonial landmarks, museums, historic streets, and traditional settlements. Each of the 25 selected sites was categorized and mapped using its precise geographic coordinates.

We systematically reviewed the historical background of each location and compared the different themes that appeared across the dataset. We also conducted strict QA verification to ensure copyright compliance for all visual assets and data source integrity before implementing the interactive mapping interface.

3. Key Findings & Spatial Analysis

From the map, it is clear that many important heritage sites are concentrated within George Town’s historic core. Locations such as Fort Cornwallis, Kapitan Keling Mosque, Khoo Kongsi, Armenian Street, St. George’s Church, and the Goddess of Mercy Temple are located close to one another. This concentration reflects the historical importance of the city centre as a place of trade, administration, religion, and migration.

The analysis also shows that Penang’s heritage extends beyond the UNESCO World Heritage area. Sites such as Batu Ferringhi Fishing Village, Balik Pulau Heritage Town, Snake Temple, and Suffolk House demonstrate that important cultural and historical landscapes can also be found in coastal and rural areas. These places help tell a broader story about everyday life, local communities, and economic activities throughout Penang’s history.

One of the strongest themes identified in the dataset is multiculturalism. The selected sites represent Malay, Chinese, Indian, Burmese, Thai, and European influences, highlighting the diverse communities that contributed to the development of George Town. Other common themes include migration, religion, trade, community identity, and colonial history.

Overall, the findings suggest that heritage in Penang is more than a collection of historic buildings. The sites are connected through shared stories of cultural exchange, settlement, and community development. By combining mapping and narrative analysis, this project provides a better understanding of how heritage is distributed across space and how different groups have contributed to the identity of George Town and Penang.
